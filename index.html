The Collection

Over 50,000 works spanning almost every major world culture, from ancient civilizations to the present day.

Collection Area

American Art

From colonial portraiture to cutting-edge contemporary work, VMFA's American art holdings trace the full sweep of artistic production in the United States.

Highlights

Thomas Cole

The Hunter's Return

1845 · Oil on canvas

Jacob Lawrence

Builders No. 1

1968 · Gouache on board

Deborah Roberts

Let Them Be Children

2018 · Collage & mixed media

Jamel Shabazz

Dapper

1980 · Photography

VMFA's American art collection spans more than three centuries, from the colonial era to the present. It includes paintings, sculpture, works on paper, photography, and decorative arts that reflect the diverse artistic traditions of the United States. The collection is particularly strong in 19th-century landscape painting, early modernism, and contemporary work by African American artists.

Collection Area

Ancient Art

Objects from Egypt, Greece, Rome, and the Near East that illuminate the art and culture of the ancient world, spanning more than 5,000 years of human creativity.

Highlights

Greek, Attic

Black-Figure Amphora

ca. 540 BCE · Ceramic

Roman

Portrait of a Young Man

2nd century CE · Marble

Egyptian

Canopic Jar with Falcon Head

ca. 664–332 BCE · Limestone

Greek

Bronze Warrior Statuette

ca. 500 BCE · Bronze

VMFA's ancient art collection encompasses works from Egypt, the Near East, Greece, Etruria, and Rome. Highlights include a remarkable group of Greek vases, Roman marble sculpture, and Egyptian objects. These works offer direct connections to the civilizations that shaped the course of Western art and culture.

Collection Area

European Art

A sweeping survey of European painting, sculpture, and decorative arts from the medieval period through the early 20th century, including the celebrated Paul and Rachel Mellon Collection.

Highlights

Claude Monet

Field of Poppies, Giverny

1885 · Oil on canvas

Hubert Robert

Architectural Fantasy

1780s · Oil on canvas

Edgar Degas

Ballet Dancer

ca. 1880 · Bronze

Pierre-Auguste Renoir

Woman with a Parasol

1876 · Oil on canvas

The European art collection at VMFA is anchored by the extraordinary Paul and Rachel Mellon Collection, one of the finest assemblages of French Impressionist painting in the American South. Works by Monet, Degas, Renoir, and Cassatt sit alongside Dutch Golden Age paintings, Italian Renaissance works, and British portraiture spanning six centuries.

Collection Area

African American Art

A powerful and continually growing collection celebrating the contributions of Black artists to American and global visual culture, from the Harlem Renaissance to the present.

Highlights

Jacob Lawrence

Catfish Row

1947 · Tempera on hardboard

Jamel Shabazz

Dapper

1980 · Photography

Radcliffe Bailey

Vessel

2012 · Mixed media sculpture

Deborah Roberts

Let Them Be Children

2018 · Collage & mixed media

VMFA's African American art collection is among the most significant at any encyclopedic museum in the American South. It spans the Harlem Renaissance, the Civil Rights era, and the contemporary moment, with works by Jacob Lawrence, Radcliffe Bailey, Deborah Roberts, Jamel Shabazz, and many others. The collection reflects VMFA's deep commitment to telling the full story of American art.

Collection Area

Modern & Contemporary Art

Works from the early 20th century to the present day that reflect the radical transformations of modernism and the rich diversity of contemporary global practice.

Highlights

Julie Mehretu

Stadia III

2004 · Ink & acrylic on canvas

Sam Gilliam

Orange Flip

1968 · Acrylic on canvas

Louise Bourgeois

Spider

1997 · Steel & mixed media

Kara Walker

Gone, An Historical Romance

1994 · Cut paper silhouette

VMFA's modern and contemporary collection encompasses painting, sculpture, photography, video, and installation art from the 1900s to the present. The collection reflects the full diversity of modern art movements — from Abstract Expressionism and Color Field painting to Minimalism, Conceptual art, and the rich landscape of contemporary global practice. Recent acquisitions have significantly strengthened holdings by artists of color and women artists.

Collection Area

Fabergé & Russian Decorative Arts

The largest public collection of Fabergé outside Russia, including five Imperial Easter Eggs — masterpieces of goldsmithing created for the Russian Imperial family.

Highlights

House of Fabergé

Imperial Peter the Great Easter Egg

1903 · Gold, enamel, diamonds, sapphires

House of Fabergé

Imperial Caucasus Easter Egg

1893 · Gold, enamel, rubies

House of Fabergé

Imperial Duchess of Marlborough Egg

1902 · Gold, enamel, diamonds

House of Fabergé

Rock Crystal Kovsh

ca. 1900 · Rock crystal, gold, diamonds

VMFA holds the largest public collection of objects by the House of Fabergé outside of Russia, including five Imperial Easter Eggs — only 50 were ever created for the Russian Imperial family. The collection was given to VMFA in 1947 by Lillian Thomas Pratt and remains one of the most beloved and visited parts of the museum. Each egg is a masterpiece of goldsmithing, enamelwork, and gemstone setting, representing the pinnacle of decorative arts in Imperial Russia.

Featured Artists & Artifacts

Explore highlights from VMFA's permanent collection of more than 50,000 works.

Photography

Dapper

Jamel Shabazz · 1980

One of the most beloved street photographers of the hip-hop era, Jamel Shabazz documented the style and dignity of Black New Yorkers with warmth and intimacy.

Mixed Media · Sculpture

Vessel

Radcliffe Bailey · 2012

Atlanta-based artist Radcliffe Bailey creates multimedia works that weave together personal memory, African diaspora history, and cosmic imagery.

Oil on Canvas · Impressionism

Field of Poppies, Giverny

Claude Monet · 1885

A cornerstone of the Paul and Rachel Mellon Collection, this radiant Impressionist landscape captures the gardens at Giverny in full bloom.

Decorative Arts · Goldsmith

Imperial Peter the Great Easter Egg

Fabergé firm · 1903

One of only 50 Imperial Easter Eggs ever created by the House of Fabergé, presented by Tsar Nicholas II to Empress Alexandra in 1903.

Painting · Contemporary

Stadia III

Julie Mehretu · 2004

Ethiopian-American artist Julie Mehretu is celebrated for her monumental abstract works that layer architectural diagrams, maps, and gestural mark-making.

Collage · Mixed Media

Let Them Be Children

Deborah Roberts · 2018

Austin-based artist Deborah Roberts creates large-scale collages that center Black childhood, joy, and innocence.
